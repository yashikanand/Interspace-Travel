Uranus

Uranus, the blue planet, is composed of water, methane and ammonia fluids. This planet, like Venus, also rotates in opposite direction to other planets. Uranus is the only planet whose equator is near to right angle to its orbit giving it most extreme seasons in the solar system. For nearly a quarter of each Uranian year, the Sun rays fall directly over both the pole creating other half of planet with dark winter for 21 years long. The faint rings around Uranus gives a unique experience for star gazing with 27 moons.
